# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the central hub for OctoAcme's project management documentation. This space provides the guidelines, processes, and artifacts that help our teams plan, execute, and ship work consistently and effectively.

## Purpose & Scope

These docs apply to all cross-functional projects that deliver product features, services, or integrations at OctoAcme. Whether you are a new team member getting up to speed or an experienced contributor looking for a reference, this documentation is your starting point.

---

## Process Summary

### Project Initiation
Validate business needs, identify stakeholders, establish measurable goals, and align on initial direction before committing to a plan. Every project starts with a lightweight one-pager that captures the problem, objective, success metrics, and proposed team.

### Planning
Break approved work into shippable increments, clarify acceptance criteria and the Definition of Done, estimate scope, and map milestones and dependencies. A kickoff meeting aligns the delivery team and stakeholders before execution begins.

### Execution & Tracking
Use agile boards and a regular cadence of standups to keep the team aligned. Quality and testing standards are applied continuously, and blockers are escalated promptly through clear channels so delivery stays on track.

### Risk Management & Communication
Maintain a simple risk register capturing impact, probability, owner, and mitigation for each identified risk. Communicate regularly with stakeholders through weekly PM/PdM syncs, twice-weekly standups, and monthly stakeholder updates. Escalate issues as needed without delay.

### Release & Deployment
Standardize release workflows and use deployment checklists to ship safely. Every release is verified against acceptance criteria before announcement, and rollback procedures are defined in advance.

### Retrospective & Continuous Improvement
After each delivery cycle or incident, capture learnings and create concrete, measurable action items. Retrospectives are blameless and focused on improving systems and processes rather than assigning fault.

### Roles & Personas
Define who does what across the project lifecycle. Core roles include Project Manager (PM), Product Manager (PdM), Developers, QA/Testing, and Stakeholders, each with clear responsibilities and communication expectations.
